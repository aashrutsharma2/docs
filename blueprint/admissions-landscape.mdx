India does not have one admissions system. It has dozens of parallel systems running simultaneously, each with its own portal, timeline, document requirements, and rules.

---

## Who Runs Admissions

Three distinct types of entities control different parts of the process.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Exam Bodies" icon="file-pen">
    Conduct entrance exams. Publish ranks and results. Do not run counselling their job ends at the rank list.\
    \\
  </Card>

  <Card title="Counselling Authorities" icon="building-2">
    Convert ranks into seat allotments. Run choice filling, allocation rounds, and upgrade cycles. Control who gets what seat and when.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Institutions" icon="school">
    Hold the actual seats. Set eligibility criteria. Handle physical reporting, document verification, and fee collection after allotment.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Counselling and admission are not the same thing. Counselling is the seat allocation process run by an authority. Admission is confirmed only after fee payment, document verification, and reporting at the institution itself.
</Note>

## Where the System Breaks

The nine-stage journey is manageable when a student is in one counselling system. Most students are not. A typical JEE Main qualifier participates in JoSAA, their state engineering counselling, and possibly one institutional counselling — simultaneously.

<Warning>
  There is no automatic coordination between counselling systems. Accepting a seat in one system does not notify or withdraw you from others. Missing a withdrawal deadline can mean losing your seat acceptance fee.
</Warning>

## The Five Breakdowns

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="Duplicate Registration" icon="copy">
    Every portal requires a fresh account with the same personal and academic details entered from scratch. No system accepts what another has already collected.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Repeated Document Upload" icon="upload">
    The same photograph, mark sheets, and certificates are uploaded independently to each counselling portal. One verification does not count for another.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Disconnected Deadlines" icon="calendar-x">
    Choice fill windows, allotment days, acceptance deadlines, and reporting cutoffs across systems do not align and are not visible in one place. The student tracks everything manually.
  </Card>

  <Card title="No Shared Status" icon="eye-off">
    Seat status in one counselling system is invisible from another. A student holding an allotment in JoSAA has no way to check their state CET status from the same interface.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Verification Repetition" icon="refresh-cw">
    Documents verified by one authority are not accepted by another. The institution then verifies them again independently. The same documents can go through four or five verification cycles.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What Superadmission Proposes

<Info>
  Superadmission is a proposed infrastructure layer that sits between students and existing counselling systems. It does not replace any authority or change how seats are allocated. It adds a shared layer for identity, documents, status tracking, and guidance — so the student stops being the coordination mechanism.
</Info>

The allocation logic of every authority remains entirely unchanged. What changes is the work the student has to do to interact with all of them.
