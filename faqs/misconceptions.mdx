---
title: 'Misconceptions'
---

**"This is just another portal."**

This comes up often, and it is understandable — India has accumulated a lot of portals over the years, many of which did not meaningfully improve on what came before. The distinction with Superadmission is architectural: a portal is a front end on an existing process; Superadmission is a shared layer beneath all the existing processes. A student who uses JoSAA and a state CET today has to create two separate identities and upload documents twice. On Superadmission, they register once and the identity travels. That is not a better portal — it is a different infrastructure model.

---

**"This will replace counselling authorities."**

Superadmission does not make allocation decisions, does not set eligibility criteria, and does not issue merit lists. Every allocation decision runs on rules configured by the counselling authority. Every eligibility call is the authority's to make. The platform provides infrastructure; the authority retains everything that constitutes their actual role.

---

**"This is a private company monetising student data."**

Superadmission's data model is the opposite of monetisation through data. The platform is designed around minimisation — collect only what is necessary, delete what is no longer needed, and ensure the student has clear visibility into what is held about them. Student data is not shared with third parties, not used for advertising, and not retained beyond the admission cycle for anything other than the student's own persistent academic record. The organisation is built with the intent of public infrastructure governance, not private data exploitation.

---

**"The algorithm is a black box."**

Every allocation has a complete decision trail. The algorithm used — a variant of the Gale-Shapley deferred acceptance algorithm — has been publicly documented since 1962 and is used in some of the most rigorous allocation systems in the world, including the US medical residency matching programme. The rules applied are configured by the counselling authority. The outcome for every student is fully traceable. A black box is, by definition, one with no visible output. This is the opposite.

---

**"This will only help students who are already well-off."**

The design explicitly targets the student who is least well-served by the current system — the first-generation applicant, the student from a smaller town, the student without a coaching centre advising them through the process. Single registration, document portability, proactive alerts, and in-built guidance are all most valuable for the student who has the fewest external resources. The platform benefits all students, but the equity dividend is largest for those who currently rely most on paid guidance and family networks to navigate a process that should be navigable without either.

---

**"This is a government project."**

Superadmission is developed by Aidra LLP, a private organisation. It is not a government project, not government-funded, and not mandated by any government body. It is built on government infrastructure (India Stack) and aligned with government policy goals (NEP 2020, NDEAR, Viksit Bharat 2047), but it is independently developed. The intent is that the platform eventually operates under a public infrastructure governance model — but it is being built by a private team.

---

**"The prototype is the product."**

The prototype demonstrates the core architecture and the critical flows. It is not the production system. It has not run a live counselling cycle at scale. Several capabilities — cross-counselling conflict detection, regional language support, the virtual spot round — are designed and partially built but not fully production-ready. The Current Stage page documents exactly what exists and what does not.

---

**"Superadmission makes the allocation decision."**

The allocation engine runs the rules that the counselling authority configures. If the authority configures a 50% OBC-NCL quota with a merit-cum-preference allocation, the engine applies exactly that. The allocation decision is the authority's — the engine executes it. Superadmission has no ability to override a counselling authority's configured rules, and no interest in doing so.
