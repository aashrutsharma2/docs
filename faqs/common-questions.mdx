title: 'Common Questions'
---

**Is Superadmission replacing my state's counselling process?**

No. Superadmission is not a replacement for any counselling authority. JoSAA, state CET cells, NEET counselling bodies — these continue to run their own processes, set their own rules, and make their own allocation decisions. Superadmission provides the shared infrastructure layer underneath: a verified student identity, a common document record, and a configurable allocation engine. The authority remains where it is. The execution becomes more efficient.

---

**Is my data safe on this platform?**

Yes — and the design is built to minimise what is collected in the first place. The Aadhaar number used for identity verification is not stored at any point. Only the APAAR ID — a non-reversible derivative — is retained. Documents are fetched via DigiLocker with the student's explicit consent and are deleted 90 days after the admission cycle ends. Payment credentials are not stored. The platform is designed around the principle that storing less reduces exposure, not just that what is stored should be protected.

Full details on data handling are in the Data Privacy Approach section.

---

**Do I need to pay to use this as a student?**

No. The student-facing registration and navigation on Superadmission carries no fee. Counselling registration fees — set by counselling authorities — and seat acceptance fees are passed through the platform but are set entirely by the authority, not by Superadmission.

---

**What if I don't have Aadhaar or DigiLocker?**

Registration requires an Aadhaar-linked mobile number for OTP verification. Students without an Aadhaar-linked number have an alternate pathway — it takes longer and involves a manual step, but it is available. DigiLocker document fetch requires a DigiLocker account; students without one can create one during registration. DigiLocker account creation is free and takes a few minutes.

---

**Is this officially recognised by the government?**

Superadmission is a privately developed platform built on India Stack's public infrastructure. It is not currently mandated or officially endorsed by any government body. It is built to NDEAR standards, it aligns with NEP 2020's goals, and its India Stack integrations comply with UIDAI, DigiLocker, and NPCI guidelines. Government recognition and partnership is what the pilot phase is designed to build toward — not something we are claiming ahead of that work.

---

**Can I use this if I'm only applying to one counselling?**

Yes. The value is proportionally higher when a student is applying to multiple counsellings — because the single-registration and document-portability benefits compound across counsellings. But for a single counselling, the platform still offers faster verification, proactive deadline alerts, Pravesh AI guidance at choice filling, and a fully explained allocation outcome. The benefit exists for one counselling; it scales with more.

---

**What happens if the platform makes an error in my allocation?**

Every allocation on Superadmission generates a complete decision trail — the inputs, the rules applied, and the outcome, step by step. If a student believes their allocation is incorrect, the trail is the starting point for review. The counselling authority adjudicates the dispute; the platform provides the evidence. If the audit trail shows an engine error, the allocation is corrected. If the audit trail shows the engine applied the configured rules correctly and the rules produced an unexpected result, the dispute is with the rules — which is the authority's domain.

---

**How is this different from the existing NIC portals?**

NIC-built portals are counselling-specific front ends — they are built for a specific counselling and they do not share data with other counsellings. Superadmission is a shared infrastructure layer that sits beneath all participating counsellings. The architectural difference is significant: NIC portals digitise the existing process; Superadmission redesigns the process at the structural level by creating a shared identity and document layer that does not exist today.

---

**Is this available in regional languages?**

A Hindi interface is in active development. Other regional languages are on the roadmap post-pilot. The current prototype operates in English. The Pravesh AI guidance layer is designed to adapt its language and complexity to context — regional language support is the next layer of that work.

---

**What happens to my data after the admissions cycle ends?**

Documents fetched via DigiLocker are deleted 90 days after the cycle closes, per DigiLocker's retention guidelines. Structured academic data — your verified scores, category, allocation outcome — is retained as part of your persistent profile, which carries forward to the next cycle if you apply again. You can request deletion of your profile at any point.
