Before UPI, India had many payment apps. Each worked. Payments were still fragmented, slow, and confusing across platforms. UPI did not build another payment app. It built the protocol underneath all of them. Today, UPI processes over 250 billion transactions annually and handles nearly 50% of global real-time payment volume.

Superadmission is the same idea — applied to higher education admissions.

## What Superadmission connects — and what it leaves alone

Counselling authorities keep full control of their own processes. Superadmission provides the coordination layer.

| Superadmission provides | Authorities retain |
|---|---|
| Shared student identity | Their eligibility rules |
| Document verification infrastructure | Their reservation mandates |
| Unified payment layer | Their seat matrices |
| Cross-counselling visibility | Their governance |
| Allocation audit trail | Their round structures |

<Tip>
The same principle that lets a UPI payment work across HDFC, SBI, and Axis Bank without requiring them to merge. Shared protocol. Separate governance.
</Tip>

## Handling India's diversity

JOSAA allocation rules differ from WBJEE. OBC creamy layer definitions vary by state. Domicile criteria change across streams. Any platform claiming to unify this has to answer one question: how do you connect systems that run by different rules?

Superadmission's answer is **interoperability, not standardisation.**

Authorities configure their specific rules — reservation mandates, category hierarchies, round structures — within the shared infrastructure. The platform connects them. It does not homogenise them.
