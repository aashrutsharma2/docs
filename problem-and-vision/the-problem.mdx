## What a student actually goes through

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register — on every portal separately">
    Same name. Same documents. Different portal. Every counselling they want to participate in requires a fresh account and a fresh start.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload — the same documents again">
    A marksheet verified in one process gets re-uploaded and re-verified in the next. Students submit the same certificates an average of 4+ times per cycle.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track — across 10 to 15 browser tabs">
    Each counselling has its own dashboard, its own round schedule, its own deadline calendar. No unified view. Students manually check each one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decide — with no information">
    No guidance on which counselling to prioritise. No explanation of what a seat offer actually means. No tool to compare options across concurrent processes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Miss — and land below their eligibility">
    A deadline conflicts with another counselling. A seat lapses. A student ends up in an institution well below what their rank could have reached.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What each stakeholder is missing

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Students">

| Today | What's missing |
|---|---|
| New registration per counselling | One identity, reused everywhere |
| Manual document upload each time | Auto-fetch from issuing source |
| No deadline coordination | Proactive conflict detection |
| No guidance at any step | Decision support built into the process |
| Allocation outcome with no explanation | Transparent, traceable results |

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Institutions">

| Today | What's missing |
|---|---|
| Re-verify the same documents | Pre-validated student records on day one |
| Fill-rate data only after a round closes | Real-time seat visibility |
| High grievance volume from document disputes | Fewer disputes by design |
| No signal on why seats go unfilled | Live withdrawal and vacancy data |

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Counselling Authorities">

| Today | What's missing |
|---|---|
| Build and maintain own tech stack | Shared infrastructure, plug in your rules |
| No visibility into other counselling processes | Ecosystem-level data within defined governance |
| Multiple rounds just to fill seats | Coordination mechanisms between processes |
| Manual audit trails | Automated, queryable logs |

  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## The guidance gap

<Tip>
An entire private industry exists to help students navigate admissions — consultants, coaching centre counselling desks, edtech platforms tracking cutoffs, parent WhatsApp groups. Every rupee spent there represents a gap the official process was never designed to fill.
</Tip>

Students with resources get guidance. Students without those resources navigate alone.

The outcome a student reaches ends up correlating with access to consultants, not just merit. That is the gap Superadmission is built to close — structurally, not through a separate scheme.

## Vacancy is a coordination failure

Most vacant seats correspond to eligible students who were active in another counselling simultaneously, missed a deadline because of overlapping windows, or could not manage the financial pressure of blocking funds across multiple processes at once.

<img src="/images/samplescreenshot.jpg" alt="Seat vacancy trend 2020 to 2025" />

**The demand exists. The coordination does not.**
