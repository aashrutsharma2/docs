## The eleven capabilities

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Cross-counselling eligibility view" icon="map">
    A student who qualifies for JoSAA, a state CET, and NEET state quota sees all of them — deadlines, seat counts, required actions — in one place. No current system does this.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Unified document vault" icon="vault">
    Submit once. Accepted everywhere that integrates. The 4.2x re-submission cycle ends at step one.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Calibrated deadline alerts" icon="bell">
    Not "counselling registration is open." An alert that tells this specific student their MHT CET choice filling closes in 36 hours — and that one document is still pending.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Strategy-proof allocation" icon="shuffle">
    Gale-Shapley stable matching. 2012 Nobel Prize in Economics. Listing genuine preferences in genuine order is always the optimal strategy. No coaching advantage. No gaming.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Virtual Spot Round" icon="circle-play">
    Live, rank-gated digital seat selection. No travel. No demand drafts. No physical queues. Remaining seats claimed digitally within the same cycle.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Plain-language seat explanation" icon="comment">
    When a seat offer arrives, Pravesh AI explains what it means — probability of upgrade, comparable alternatives at the same rank, what accepting vs. floating implies — before the student decides.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Unified confirmation and payment" icon="check-circle">
    Seat acceptance and fee payment in one flow. Regardless of which counselling the seat came from.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Real-time vacancy visibility" icon="chart-bar">
    Students, institutions, and authorities all see the same vacancy picture — updated as confirmations and withdrawals process. Not a snapshot. Live.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Configurable allocation engine" icon="sliders">
    Each authority defines their own seat matrix, reservation rules, round structure. The engine runs it — with full auditability — without requiring custom software development.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Student-readable audit trail" icon="file-magnifying-glass">
    Every allocation decision is logged: rank applied, preferences considered, rules used, outcome. If a student questions why they received a particular seat, the answer exists and is theirs to see.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Reserved seat auto-reallocation" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Unfilled reserved seats are reprocessed within the same cycle before they lapse. The coordination failure that leaves reserved seats empty is structurally addressed.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Why this matters

### For students who are doing this for the first time

The single largest equity dividend of Superadmission is not visible in any one feature. It is in the aggregate effect of removing the process knowledge advantage.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Today" icon="xmark">
    A student whose family has no prior higher education experience navigates a process that a well-networked student has already been coached through. Same rank. Different outcome probability — because one knows what to do and the other is learning as they go, often too late.
  </Card>
  <Card title="With Superadmission" icon="check">
    Process knowledge is embedded in the platform. The guidance layer explains what choice filling means before a student starts filling choices. The information advantage that money and connections currently provide is built in as standard.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

**95.5% of India's 15-29 age group own smartphones. 95% of villages have 3G/4G access.** The infrastructure conditions to reach every student exist. The equity gap is now a design problem, not an access problem.

---

### For institutions

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Early vacancy signals" icon="radar">
    As allocation rounds proceed and withdrawals happen in real time, institutions see their vacancy picture forming before a round closes — not after. Faster decisions. Fewer lapsed seats.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cleaner incoming data" icon="database">
    Students with a verified Superadmission profile arrive with documents already checked against primary sources. The institution receives a structured record. Staff hours move from document-handling to everything else.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Our research: institutions spend an average of **4,500 staff hours per admission cycle**. Even a 20% reduction in document-handling overhead is a significant operational saving at scale.

---

### For allocation integrity

When students believe the allocation process has workarounds — that connected students get better results — the social contract around merit-based admissions erodes.

The audit trail changes this. Allocation decisions are verifiable. Not just to the student. To any party with legitimate access to the record. A counselling authority can verify the engine applied their rules correctly. A student can see the exact inputs and sequence that produced their result.

> **This is not about catching fraud. It is about making the question of whether fraud occurred answerable. That alone changes behaviour at every level of the system.**

---

### For India's DPI story

India Stack — Aadhaar, UPI, DigiLocker, ONDC — has become the most referenced example of what digital public infrastructure can do. UPI alone handled 228 billion transactions in 2025. India championed this model at G20.

Higher education admissions is a sector India Stack has not yet reached. There is no UPI for admissions. Superadmission is designed to be that layer — built on the same India Stack foundations, following the same interoperability principles.

If it works, it extends a proven model into a sector that needs it. If it scales, it becomes a template for other competitive allocation systems in India — scholarships, postgraduate admissions, government housing, healthcare referrals — where the same coordination problem exists.
