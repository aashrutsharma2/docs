title: "Constraints and Dependencies"
description: "What Superadmission's development and adoption depend on — factors that are real, external, and not fully within our control."
---

Building infrastructure is not the same as building a product. A product can be shipped to users who choose to adopt it. Infrastructure requires the participation of the actors whose systems it is meant to connect — and those actors have their own timelines, priorities, and constraints.

This page documents the dependencies that shape Superadmission's development honestly. None of these are reasons the project should not exist. They are the conditions it has to be built within.

---

## Counselling authority participation is not optional

Superadmission cannot be adopted unilaterally by students. A student who registers on the platform still needs to participate in counsellings run by authorities — and those authorities need to integrate with Superadmission for the platform's core value to materialise.

This is the most fundamental dependency. The platform's design is sound. Its utility, at scale, requires counselling authorities to see enough value in integration to commit the time and resources the integration requires. That is a stakeholder engagement challenge as much as a technical one, and it cannot be resolved by improving the product alone.

The pilot strategy is designed around this constraint: start with authorities where the benefit case is clearest, demonstrate measurable impact, and let demonstrated outcomes drive broader adoption. But the dependency is real.

## India Stack API reliability and coverage

Superadmission's verification layer is built on Aadhaar eKYC, DigiLocker's Requester API, and APAAR's federation API. These integrations work well when the APIs are available and when students have Aadhaar-linked mobile numbers and DigiLocker accounts.

The gaps:
- Students without an Aadhaar-linked mobile number cannot complete OTP verification — the platform has a fallback pathway, but it is slower and requires manual intervention
- DigiLocker's document coverage is comprehensive for CBSE and many state boards, but thinner for some state-level issuing authorities — particularly income and domicile certificates from smaller district offices
- APAAR generation is ongoing nationally but not yet universal — students not yet on APAAR require an alternate identifier pathway

None of these are blockers for a pilot. At national scale, they require either workarounds or improvements to the underlying India Stack infrastructure that are outside our control.

## The DPDP Act 2023 implementation timeline

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 is the regulatory framework governing how Superadmission handles student data. The Act's provisions — consent architecture, data minimisation, localisation, breach notification — are reflected in the platform's design.

What is not yet clear is the pace and specifics of secondary regulation: the Data Protection Board's operational framework, sectoral guidelines for education data, and enforcement timelines. These could require changes to the platform's data handling architecture as they are clarified. Designing for DPDP compliance today, before all secondary regulation is published, involves interpretation of what the Act requires — and that interpretation may need to be revisited.

This is not unique to Superadmission. Every Indian digital platform faces the same uncertainty. But for a platform handling sensitive data for crores of students, the stakes of getting it wrong are higher than for most.

## Political economy of interoperability

Counselling authorities are not purely technical actors. They are institutions with their own stakeholders, budgets, political relationships, and institutional interests. A state CET cell that has invested in its own technology infrastructure has a different disposition toward integrating with shared infrastructure than one that is running on legacy systems and has limited technical capacity.

Some counselling authorities will see Superadmission's interoperability design as a resource. Others may see it as a change management challenge, or as a question of how it affects their authority over their own process. These are legitimate concerns and they are not resolved by having the right technical design.

Navigating this requires building relationships with the right stakeholders at the right levels — not just demonstrating the product. This is time-consuming and cannot be shortcut. It is a real constraint on adoption timelines.

---

## Internet and device access

The platform is optimised for mobile devices and lower-bandwidth connections. But "optimised" is not the same as "accessible everywhere." Students in areas with limited or unreliable 3G connectivity, or using very low-end devices, will have a meaningfully different experience than students with stable 4G and a mid-range phone.

95.15% of Indian villages had 3G or 4G access as of 2024. That leaves a non-trivial tail — and among the students most likely to be in that tail are exactly the students the platform most needs to serve. Full reach is a longer-horizon challenge than a first pilot.
