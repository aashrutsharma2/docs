## Infrastructure and Scope

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does Superadmission replace existing national or state allocation authorities?">
    It does not. Allocation authorities like JoSAA, JAC, and state CET cells retain absolute sovereignty over their processes. They configure seat matrices, define eligibility rules, and execute their distinct allocation algorithms.

    Superadmission acts strictly as an underlying coordination layer. It unifies the fragmented user touchpoints. Students construct one verified profile, and the infrastructure routes their cryptographic identity and documents to the respective independent authorities they choose to apply to. The authorities see fully verified candidates. The fundamental mechanics of their internal allocation remain untouched.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the architectural distinction between Superadmission and existing counselling portals?">
    Existing portals function as isolated vertical silos. A candidate applying to three different jurisdictions must create three accounts, upload identical documents three times, and monitor three disconnected timelines.

    Superadmission introduces a horizontal protocol layer beneath these verticals. Comparable to how UPI functions as a unified gateway for disparate banking systems, Superadmission serves as a unified gateway for independent admissions systems. The existing portals can remain operational; however, Superadmission ensures the underlying data travels securely and efficiently between them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the commercial model for end-users?">
    Core platform access, profile creation, and document verification are completely fee-free for students. Statutory registration and seat acceptance fees mandated by the respective counselling authorities are processed directly through the platform's payment gateway and settled with the authorities. Superadmission applies zero markup to state or national counselling fees.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does the system accommodate edge cases, such as unlinked or fragmented identity documents?">
    The primary identity verification layer utilises standard digital public infrastructure protocols, requiring linked mobile authentication. However, the architecture anticipates document fragmentation. For candidates lacking aligned digital records, the system provides a structured manual verification fallback. This ensures the infrastructure accommodates the entire spectrum of applicant readiness, capturing edge cases without halting the central flow.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Technical Operations & Intelligence

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is the exact scope of PraveshAI's authority in the allocation process?">
    PraveshAI operates strictly as an intelligence surface. It holds zero write-access to seat matrices and cannot alter, influence, or override an allocation algorithm.

    Its function is purely analytical. It processes a candidate's verified parameters against historical cutoff data and real-time round dynamics to surface probabilistic outcomes. It translates complex allotment data into actionable guidance, allowing candidates to understand the precise implications of actions like freeze, float, or slide before they execute them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does the infrastructure sustain concurrent load during national round publications?">
    Round publications generate the most severe traffic spikes in the academic calendar. The platform architecture decouples the stateful allocation engine from the public-facing distribution layer. Allocation results are pre-computed, encrypted, and distributed across a global Content Delivery Network (CDN) with read replicas. This approach neutralises database bottlenecks, ensuring high availability even under extreme, multi-million concurrent user loads.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does the integration surface look like for a participating state authority?">
    The standard integration relies on a versioned, deeply documented REST API schema. This allows state systems to consume pre-verified candidate data programmatically. For authorities operating legacy systems unable to support direct API consumption in the short term, we provide secure, file-based batch adapters to bridge the transition.

    For detailed technical specifications and API documentation access, please connect via [Talk to Founders](https://cal.com/aashrut/talk-to-founders).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How are algorithmic discrepancies or guidance errors handled?">
    Transparency is built into the base layer. Every recommendation generated by PraveshAI and every algorithmic allocation is accompanied by a cryptographically secure, append-only audit log. If an outcome is challenged, the log exposes the precise rules and data points that triggered the decision. This transforms opaque, frustrating errors into highly diagnosable and correctable anomalies.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Data Governance & Security

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is the data retention lifecycle for candidate records?">
    Data storage is ephemeral by design and aligned with the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act. Raw documents retrieved via issuing authorities are purged 90 days following the closure of the admissions cycle. Only essential structured data required for future cycle acceleration is retained, and candidates retain the perpetual right to trigger a total profile deletion at any moment.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is candidate telemetry commercialised or shared with third parties?">
    Absolutely not. Superadmission operates on a zero-trust data sharing model regarding external commercial entities. Candidate data is never licensed, sold, or exposed to coaching institutes, advertisers, or third-party publishers. The only entities granted read-access to a candidate's data are the candidate themselves and the specific statutory authorities they explicitly authorise during the application flow.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How is liability structured around cryptographically verified documents?">
    The platform's integration with digital public infrastructure confirms the strict provenance of a document. It mathematically guarantees that a document was issued by the stated authority and has not been tampered with. It does not, however, underwrite the underlying facts. If an authority issues a certificate based on false premises, the digital verification will still pass. Liability for eligibility and post-admission fraud remains within the penal framework of the respective counselling authority.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
