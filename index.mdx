## How it works

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="For Students">
    **One registration. Every counselling.**

    A student verifies their identity once via Aadhaar. Documents are fetched at source via DigiLocker. Every participating counselling reads from the same verified record. No re-entry, no re-upload, no couriered envelopes.

    Pravesh AI explains seat offers in plain language, surfaces comparable options at the student's rank, and guides choice filling with probability estimates, at the moment the student needs it, not after.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Aadhaar eKYC">
        Identity verified once. Accepted across all participating counsellings.
      </Step>
      <Step title="DigiLocker document fetch">
        Documents pulled from primary sources. No uploads, no photocopies.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Unified choice filling">
        One dashboard. All active counsellings. Deadlines tracked automatically.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Pravesh AI at decision time">
        Personalised probability estimates and plain-language seat explanations during choice filling.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="For Counselling Authorities">
    **Your rules. Shared infrastructure.**

    Each authority configures their own seat matrix, reservation rules, and round structure. The engine runs it with full auditability. No custom software development required.

    - Students arrive with documents already confirmed against primary sources. Manual verification queues reduce significantly.
    - Seat confirmation and withdrawal data updates continuously across all rounds. Authorities see vacancy forming as it happens.
    - Reserved seats are automatically reprocessed within the same cycle before they lapse.
    - Every allocation decision carries a full audit trail: rank applied, preferences considered, rules used, visible and contestable.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## What is genuinely new here

These capabilities do not exist anywhere in Indian admissions today.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="One profile, all counsellings">
    A student profile verified once, portable across all participating counsellings, central and state, without re-submission. Not a workaround. A structural change to how identity and documents flow through the system.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Calibrated deadline alerts">
    Proactive alerts tied to each student's specific registered counsellings, not broadcast announcements. A student registered for JoSAA and JAC Delhi gets deadlines relevant to them, surfaced at the right moment.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Reserved seat automatic reallocation">
    Unfilled reserved seats are reprocessed within the same cycle before they lapse. This addresses the coordination failure, not by changing reservation policy, but by fixing the mechanism that fails to execute it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Virtual Spot Round">
    Digitally conducted, rank-gated, without physical attendance or demand drafts. Spot round participation has historically excluded students who cannot travel. This removes that barrier.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Full allocation audit trail">
    Rank applied, preferences considered, rules used, per student, per round, visible and contestable. Not a log. A transparency mechanism.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Policy alignment

<Note>
  Superadmission is not built to comply with policy. It is built as an operationalisation of it.
</Note>

| Framework | What it activates |
| --- | --- |
| **NEP 2020** | Unified, digital, equitable admissions. APAAR and ABC activated at seat confirmation. |
| **NDEAR** | Interoperability-first design. No authority is locked into a proprietary system. |
| **Digital India** | The highest-frequency citizen-education interaction becomes genuinely digital, not a digitised form. |
| **IndiaAI Mission** | Pravesh AI is transparent, student-serving, and non-prescriptive. No black-box recommendations. |
| **DPDP Act 2023** | Consent-first. Aadhaar numbers never stored. Data stays on Indian soil. |
| **Viksit Bharat 2047** | Merit-based access to higher education, without a process that advantages those who already know it. |
