This blueprint is written for people who need to understand Superadmission at depth not at the level of a summary, but at the level at which serious decisions get made.

Different readers will enter this document with different questions. This page is a guide to finding what is most relevant to you.

---

## Researchers and policy analysts

If you are studying India's higher education system, admission infrastructure, or digital public goods, this document is intended to be a primary resource. The problem section is grounded in primary research conducted across 5,247 students, 218 institutions, and 18 states between September and December 2024. The data is cited with source notes and distinguishes between figures drawn from official datasets (AISHE, UDISE\+, Ministry of Education) and figures generated through our own fieldwork.

We have deliberately included the methodology behind our research, the questions we could not answer, and the limitations of what we found. That is not standard practice in product documentation. We think it should be.

---

## Institutions and counselling authorities

If you are an institution evaluating whether Superadmission is relevant to your admission process, or a counselling authority exploring whether a pilot is feasible, the sections most relevant to you are How It Works, Systems That Power It, and Pilots and Adoption.

The pilot section in particular is written to be operational — it describes not just what a pilot involves but what it measures, what it requires from your side, and how outcomes are compared against your existing baseline. We are not asking you to take outcomes on faith.

---

## Government officials and policy stakeholders

India's policy environment for digital public infrastructure has advanced significantly APAAR, DigiLocker, and the broader India Stack provide the foundation that makes a system like Superadmission technically feasible today in a way it was not five years ago.

If you are working within that environment at the state or central level; this document is written to connect Superadmission's design to that policy context explicitly. The sections on Infrastructure and Integrations and Alignment with National Initiatives are directly relevant. We are not claiming to replace government systems. We are proposing to connect them more effectively for students.

---

## Investors and institutional funders

The organisation and product sections of this blueprint contain an honest account of what exists today, what is being built, what depends on adoption, and what we are not claiming. We have deliberately avoided the language of projections and timelines we cannot substantiate.

What we can show is: a validated problem at national scale, a coherent system design grounded in research, an infrastructure approach that has no direct precedent in India, and a team that has done the field work to understand this problem from the ground up.
