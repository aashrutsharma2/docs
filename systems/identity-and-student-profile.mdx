Research on manual data entry in high-volume digital systems puts error rates between 1 and 5 percent. At a crore students filling forms per cycle, that is 1 to 5 lakh processing errors — each capable of flagging an application, delaying verification, or blocking a seat.

Superadmission replaces manual entry with authorisation of data use. The student confirms. The system fetches.

---

## How it works

<img src="/images/samplescreenshot.jpg" alt="Profile formation flow from Aadhaar to Superadmission ID" />

<Steps>
  <Step title="Aadhaar login">
    Student authenticates via Aadhaar OTP. Identity confirmed at source. No form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="DigiLocker consent">
    One-time consent. Documents fetched directly from the authorities that issued them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pravesh AI validates">
    Every fetched document cross-referenced against declared details instantly. Inconsistencies flagged before the student proceeds — not weeks later when a counselling's verification window has already closed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Superadmission ID issued">
    A unique persistent ID, anchored to Aadhaar and APAAR. Recognised by every participating counselling. Never recreated.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Manually uploaded documents

Not every certificate lives on DigiLocker. Migration certificates, income declarations, disability certificates from district medical boards — these need uploading. For every manual upload, Pravesh AI runs an OCR-based analysis instantly.

<img src="/images/samplescreenshot.jpg" alt="Document confidence score interface" />

| Score | What it means | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 80 to 100 | Clean read. Fields match. Structure consistent. | Minimal officer review needed |
| 50 to 79 | Minor inconsistencies. Some fields unclear. | Specific fields highlighted for officer |
| Below 50 | Significant anomalies detected. | Priority review. Full flag summary attached. |

The confidence score organises the officer's queue — lowest first, each document pre-annotated with exactly what Pravesh AI found. Officers are not reading documents blind. They are reviewing flagged fields with context already provided.
