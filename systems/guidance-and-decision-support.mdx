There is an entire private industry that exists because admissions provides no guidance. Coaching institutes offer counselling as a separate product. Edtech platforms produce weekly content analysing cutoff trends. Consultants charge Rs 5,000 to Rs 2 lakh to help families interpret their options.

Pravesh AI is that guidance for every student, built into the process, at no additional cost.

---

## What Pravesh AI does

Pravesh AI runs through the entire admission journey. It is not a chatbot. It is the intelligence layer woven into every step where a decision needs to be made.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Eligibility checking" icon="circle-check">

  <Card title="Probability estimation" icon="chart-line">

## Choice filling what it looks like

![Choice filling interface with probability estimates and cutoff data](/images/samplescreenshot.jpg)

Students filter institutions by location, course, rank range, and fees. For each option:

- 5 years of category-wise historical cutoff data
- Live seat availability; updated as the round progresses
- Pravesh AI probability band; a reasoned range, not a confident point estimate
- Plain-language explanation of what the estimate is based on

The student can adjust their preference order and see how probability estimates shift in real time. They can test trade-offs before committing to anything.

## The strategy-proof design

In the current system, students are routinely advised to list a safer option as their first preference not their actual first choice. The reasoning is rational within an uncertain system. But it degrades match quality system-wide. Students end up below their potential. Institutions fill with students who did not prefer them.

Superadmission's allocation engine changes this. The Deferred Acceptance algorithm is mathematically strategy-proof: a student cannot improve their outcome by misrepresenting their preferences.

<Tip>
  When the system is strategy-proof, honest preference expression is always optimal. Pravesh AI's job shifts from helping students game the system to helping them understand their genuine options.
</Tip>

## The 2 AM scenario

A student has a state counselling offer expiring at midnight. A JEE Main round result is expected tomorrow. They need to decide whether to accept now or wait. It is 2 AM. Every help desk is closed. The counselling PDF does not address this specific situation.

**Today:** the decision is made alone, with incomplete information, under time pressure. Many students make the wrong call.

**On Superadmission:** Pravesh AI flagged this conflict 48 hours earlier. The student was shown the specific trade-off what happens if they accept, what happens if they wait, what the policy implications of each choice are. The decision is still theirs. They are making it with full information.
