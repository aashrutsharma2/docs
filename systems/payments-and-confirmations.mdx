There is a financial dimension to admissions that rarely gets discussed: seat acceptance fees, counselling registration charges, and refundable deposits that are technically refundable but not immediately so. A student participating in multiple counsellings simultaneously may be required to block Rs 50,000 to Rs 2 lakh across different systems within the same 2 to 3 week window.

For families with high liquidity, this is manageable. For most, it quietly shapes decisions.

Superadmission's payment layer is built to remove that pressure from the process.

## Seat acceptance

Student accepts or declines within a 48-hour window.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Student accepts">
    One tap. Confirmed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cross-counselling release — automatic">
    The system simultaneously withdraws the student from all concurrent allotments across every participating counselling. Seats released in real time across all processes. No blocking, no manual process, no waiting for the next round.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Payment via UPI">
    Confirmation in seconds. Auto-generated receipt tied to the specific allotment — student ID, counselling, institution, course, category, seat type. India's sovereign payment infrastructure only.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Seat blocking — where a student holds positions across multiple counsellings pending a decision — is one of the main drivers of vacancy in late rounds. Automatic simultaneous release on acceptance eliminates this structurally, not case by case.
</Tip>

## Physical reporting

The student arrives at the institution. Staff scan the QR code. The system confirms in real time:

- Payment received
- Allotment verified
- No duplicate admission detected

Physical documents are cross-checked against DigiLocker-fetched records for audit purposes. Not for primary verification — that already happened.

<Warning>
The QR code is single-use and time-limited. It cannot be duplicated or transferred. Once scanned at the institution, it expires.
</Warning>

## The unified payment design

In the current system, payments are tied to individual counselling portals. Each has its own gateway, its own refund process, and its own transaction timeline. Students track multiple payment statuses across disconnected systems while managing overlapping deadlines.

On Superadmission, payments are tied to the student's participation in the overall admission lifecycle. A single coordinated financial flow — not multiple disconnected transactions.

| Current system | Superadmission |
|---|---|
| Separate gateway per counselling | Unified payment layer |
| Multiple simultaneous deposits required | Coordinated flow — no parallel blocking |
| Refund timelines vary and are opaque | Transparent status, governed timelines |
| Transaction reconciliation manual | Automated, auditable |
| Time from acceptance to confirmed admission: days to weeks | Under 2 minutes |
